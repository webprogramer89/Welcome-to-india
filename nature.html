Nature Spots in India

Himalayan Region

Explore the breathtaking beauty of the Himalayan range, including places like Manali, Shimla, and Leh-Ladakh.

Kerala Backwaters

Cruise through the serene backwaters of Kerala, surrounded by lush greenery.

Andaman and Nicobar Islands

Enjoy pristine beaches, coral reefs, and dense forests in this tropical paradise.

Rann of Kutch

Witness the surreal white salt desert during the Rann Utsav festival.

Jim Corbett National Park

India's oldest national park is famous for its tigers and diverse wildlife.

Sundarbans

Home to the Royal Bengal Tiger, this mangrove forest is a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Valley of Flowers

This UNESCO World Heritage Site in Uttarakhand bursts with colorful flowers during the monsoon season.

Munnar

A hill station in Kerala known for its tea plantations and lush landscapes.

Goa Beaches

Enjoy the sun and sea at popular beaches like Baga, Anjuna, and Palolem.

Western Ghats

A UNESCO World Heritage Site, these mountains offer stunning biodiversity and trekking opportunities.

Rishikesh and Haridwar

Along the banks of the Ganges River, these cities are known for spiritual and adventure tourism.

Gir Forest National Park

The only natural habitat of the Asiatic lion in India.

Hampi

Explore the unique rock formations and ancient ruins in this UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Pangong Lake

Located in Ladakh, this high-altitude lake is known for its ever-changing shades of blue.

Tawang

Visit the scenic Tawang Monastery and enjoy the beauty of Arunachal Pradesh.
